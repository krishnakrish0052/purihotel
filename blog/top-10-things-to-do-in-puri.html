Top 10 Things to Do in Puri

Published: April 21, 2025 | 10 min read | By Puri Beach Resort Team

Puri, one of India's most sacred cities and a popular beach destination, offers a perfect blend of spirituality, culture, and natural beauty. From the magnificent Jagannath Temple to the pristine beaches, there's something for every traveler. Whether you're a devotee seeking spiritual blessings, a beach lover, or a culture enthusiast, Puri has countless experiences waiting for you.

Pro Tip: Stay at Puri Beach Resort on Marine Drive for easy access to all major attractions. Our beachfront location puts you right in the heart of Puri's best experiences!

1. Visit the Magnificent Jagannath Temple

Spiritual Experience

The Jagannath Temple, one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites, is the crown jewel of Puri. Dedicated to Lord Jagannath (a form of Lord Vishnu), this 12th-century temple is an architectural marvel and a living testament to India's rich spiritual heritage.

Best Time: Early morning (5-6 AM) for the Mangala Aarti or evening for the Sandhya Aarti

Dress Code: Traditional Indian attire recommended, no shorts or sleeveless tops

2. Relax at Swargadwar Beach

Beach Paradise

Swargadwar Beach, literally meaning "Gateway to Heaven," is Puri's most famous beach. Known for its golden sands and stunning sunrises, this beach offers the perfect setting for relaxation. Puri Beach Resort is located right on this beach, giving our guests direct access to this paradise.

Activities: Swimming, sunbathing, beach volleyball, camel rides

Best Time: Early morning for sunrise or evening for sunset

3. Witness the Rath Yatra Festival

Cultural Extravaganza

The Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival) is Puri's most famous event, attracting millions of devotees worldwide. Watch as massive decorated chariots carrying Lord Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra are pulled through the streets by devotees. This spectacular festival typically occurs in June-July.

Duration: 9 days

Tip: Book accommodation months in advance during this period

4. Day Trip to Konark Sun Temple

UNESCO World Heritage Site

Located just 35 km from Puri, the Konark Sun Temple is a 13th-century architectural masterpiece dedicated to the Sun God. The temple is designed as a colossal chariot with 12 pairs of elaborately carved wheels, drawn by seven horses. The intricate sculptures depict various aspects of life.

Distance: 35 km from Puri (1 hour drive)

Best Time: Early morning or late afternoon to avoid heat

5. Explore Chilika Lake

Asia's Largest Brackish Water Lagoon

Chilika Lake, about 50 km from Puri, is a paradise for bird watchers and nature lovers. This vast lagoon is home to over 160 species of birds, including the famous Irrawaddy dolphins. Take a boat ride to explore the lake and visit Nalabana Island, a bird sanctuary.

Distance: 50 km from Puri (1.5 hours drive)

Best Time: November to February for migratory birds

6. Visit Puri Beach Market

Local Shopping

The Puri Beach Market is a vibrant hub for shopping local handicrafts, souvenirs, and beachwear. From colorful seashell crafts to traditional Pattachitra paintings, you'll find unique items to take home. Don't forget to try the local street food!

What to Buy: Seashell crafts, Pattachitra paintings, handloom textiles

Timing: Evening is best when the market comes alive

7. Enjoy Water Sports at Puri Beach

Adventure Activities

Puri Beach offers various water sports for adventure enthusiasts. Try surfing, banana boat rides, jet skiing, or parasailing. The calm waters during winter months (October-March) are perfect for these activities. Equipment rental and trained instructors are available at the beach.

Best Season: October to March

Safety: Always use certified operators and wear life jackets

8. Visit Gundicha Temple

Aunt's Home of Lord Jagannath

Located 3 km from the Jagannath Temple, Gundicha Temple is where the deities stay during the Rath Yatra festival. Known as the "Gundicha Ghar" or Aunt's Home, this temple has beautiful architecture and a peaceful atmosphere. It's less crowded than the main temple, offering a serene spiritual experience.

Distance: 3 km from Jagannath Temple

Significance: Lord Jagannath's aunt's home, visited during Rath Yatra

9. Explore the Marine Drive

Scenic Coastal Road

The Marine Drive is a picturesque coastal road that runs along Puri's beach. Perfect for morning walks, evening strolls, or cycling, this road offers stunning views of the Bay of Bengal. Puri Beach Resort is located on this beautiful road, making it easy for our guests to enjoy these scenic views.

Best Time: Early morning for sunrise or evening for sunset

Activities: Walking, cycling, photography

10. Savor Local Odisha Cuisine

Culinary Delights

No trip to Puri is complete without tasting authentic Odisha cuisine. Try the famous Mahaprasad from the Jagannath Temple, fresh seafood at beach shacks, and traditional dishes like Dalma and Pakhala. Puri Beach Resort's multi-cuisine restaurant serves delicious local and international dishes.

Must-Try: Mahaprasad, Dalma, Pakhala, Chhena Poda

Where: Temple, beach shacks, and local restaurants

Best Time to Visit Puri

The ideal time to visit Puri is from October to March when the weather is pleasant (15°C to 28°C). This period is perfect for temple visits, beach activities, and sightseeing. The monsoon season (July-September) brings the famous Rath Yatra festival, while summer (March-June) is hot but offers spiritual significance for devotees.

Travel Tips for Puri

Getting Around

  • Auto-rickshaws: Convenient for short distances
  • Cycle rickshaws: Eco-friendly and budget-friendly
  • Taxis: Available for day trips to Konark and Chilika
  • Rental scooters: Available for independent exploration

What to Pack

  • Light cotton clothes for summer
  • Light woolens for winter
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses
  • Traditional attire for temple visits

Safety Tips

  • Swim only in designated safe zones
  • Keep valuables secure at the beach
  • Stay hydrated, especially in summer
  • Use authorized guides for temple visits
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How many days are needed to explore Puri?

A: 2-3 days are sufficient for a basic Puri trip covering the main temple, beach, and nearby attractions like Konark.

Q: Is Puri safe for solo female travelers?

A: Yes, Puri is generally safe for solo female travelers. However, basic precautions should be taken, especially at the beach during late hours.

Q: What is the best time to visit Puri for beach activities?

A: October to March is ideal for beach activities due to pleasant weather and calm sea conditions.

Q: Can non-Hindus visit the Jagannath Temple?

A: Non-Hindus are not allowed inside the main temple but can view it from outside and participate in the Rath Yatra festival.
