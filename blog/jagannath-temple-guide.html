Complete Guide to Jagannath Temple Puri

Published: April 21, 2025 | 12 min read | By Puri Beach Resort Team

The Jagannath Temple in Puri is one of the most sacred Hindu pilgrimage sites in India and one of the Char Dham (four holy abodes) that every devout Hindu aspires to visit. Dedicated to Lord Jagannath (a form of Lord Vishnu), this magnificent 12th-century temple is an architectural marvel and a living testament to India's rich spiritual heritage. Whether you're a devotee seeking blessings or a traveler interested in culture and history, this comprehensive guide will help you make the most of your visit.

History and Significance

The Jagannath Temple was built in the 12th century by King Anantavarman Chodaganga Deva of the Ganga dynasty. The temple is dedicated to Lord Jagannath, along with his brother Balabhadra and sister Subhadra. Unlike other Hindu temples where the deities are made of stone or metal, the deities here are made of wood and are replaced every 12-19 years in a ceremony known as Nabakalebara.

The temple is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites, along with Badrinath, Dwarka, and Rameswaram. It's also part of the Char Dham of Eastern India, alongside Konark Sun Temple and Lingaraj Temple in Bhubaneswar. The temple's significance is further enhanced by its association with the famous Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival), which attracts millions of devotees annually.

Temple Architecture

Architectural Marvel

The Jagannath Temple is built in the Kalinga style of architecture, featuring a towering spira (shikhara) that rises to 214 feet. The temple complex covers an area of over 400,000 square feet and is surrounded by a high wall ( Meghanada Pacheri). The main temple structure consists of four parts:

  • Deula (Sanctum): Houses the main deities
  • Jagamohan (Assembly Hall): Where devotees gather
  • Nata Mandir (Dancing Hall): For dance performances
  • Bhoga Mandap (Offering Hall): Where prasad is distributed

The Deities

Lord Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra

The temple houses three main deities:

  • Lord Jagannath: The presiding deity, representing Lord Vishnu or Krishna
  • Lord Balabhadra: Jagannath's brother, representing Balarama
  • Goddess Subhadra: Jagannath's sister

The deities are unique in their appearance – they have large eyes, no arms, and no legs. According to legend, this form was chosen based on a dream of King Indradyumna, who was instructed to build the temple in this specific form.

Temple Timings

Daily Schedule

The temple follows a strict daily schedule with various rituals performed throughout the day:

  • Mangala Aarti: 5:00 AM – Morning awakening ceremony
  • Mailam: 6:00 AM – Changing of deities' clothes
  • Abakash: 6:30 AM – Morning prayers
  • Sahanamela: 7:00 AM – Public viewing (first time)
  • Gopala Ballava Bhoga: 8:00 AM – Breakfast offering
  • Sakala Dhupa: 10:00 AM – Morning offering
  • Madhyanha Dhupa: 1:00 PM – Midday offering
  • Sandhya Aarti: 7:00 PM – Evening prayers (most popular)
  • Badasinghar Bhoga: 9:30 PM – Night offering
  • Shayan Dhupa: 10:30 PM – Deities' bedtime ritual

Note: Timings may vary during festivals and special occasions. It's advisable to check the current schedule before visiting.

Dress Code and Entry Rules

Important Rules to Follow

The Jagannath Temple has strict dress codes and entry rules that all visitors must follow:

  • Dress Code: Traditional Indian attire is mandatory. Men should wear dhoti or pants with shirts/t-shirts. Women should wear sarees, salwar kameez, or long skirts. Shorts, sleeveless tops, and Western clothing are not allowed.
  • Footwear: Shoes and slippers must be removed before entering. Shoe storage facilities are available at the entrance.
  • Non-Hindus: Non-Hindus are not allowed inside the main temple sanctum but can view the temple from outside and participate in the Rath Yatra festival.
  • Photography: Photography is strictly prohibited inside the temple complex.
  • Mobile Phones: Mobile phones are not allowed inside the temple. Storage facilities are available at the entrance.
  • Possessions: Leather items, including belts and wallets, are not allowed inside.

Major Festivals

Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival)

The Most Famous Festival

The Rath Yatra is the most significant festival at the Jagannath Temple, typically held in June-July. During this 9-day festival, the deities are taken out of the temple on massive decorated chariots and pulled through the streets by millions of devotees. The chariots travel to the Gundicha Temple, where the deities stay for 9 days before returning to the main temple.

Key Highlights:

  • Three massive chariots: Nandighosa (Jagannath), Taladhwaja (Balabhadra), Darpadalana (Subhadra)
  • Millions of devotees participate
  • Live telecast worldwide
  • Non-Hindus can participate and view the deities

Snana Yatra (Bathing Festival)

Sacred Bathing Ceremony

Snana Yatra, held in June, involves the ceremonial bathing of the deities with 108 pots of sacred water. After this ritual, the deities fall ill and are kept in isolation for 15 days (Anasara period) before reappearing in a new form.

Chandan Yatra

21-Day Festival

Held in April-May, Chandan Yatra is a 21-day festival where the deities are taken on a boat ride in the Narendra Tank. This festival marks the beginning of the construction of the chariots for Rath Yatra.

The Mahaprasad

Sacred Food Offering

The Mahaprasad of Jagannath Temple is world-famous and is considered sacred by devotees. The prasad is cooked in the temple kitchen (Rosa Ghar) using traditional methods and is distributed to thousands of devotees daily. The kitchen is said to be the largest in the world, capable of feeding 100,000 people daily.

Popular Items:

  • Khichdi: Rice and lentil preparation
  • Dalma: Mixed vegetable curry
  • Khata: Sweet and sour curry
  • Santula: Mixed vegetable dish
  • Pitha: Various sweet preparations

Important: The Mahaprasad is vegetarian and is prepared without onion and garlic. It's believed that the food is blessed by Lord Jagannath himself.

The temple is approximately 2 km from Puri Railway Station. Auto-rickshaws and cycle rickshaws are readily available outside the station.

From Bus Stand

The temple is about 1.5 km from the main bus stand. Auto-rickshaws are the most convenient mode of transport.

Best Time to Visit

For Temple Visit: Early morning (5-6 AM) for Mangala Aarti or evening (7-8 PM) for Sandhya Aarti. These times offer a spiritual atmosphere with fewer crowds.

For Festivals: June-July for Rath Yatra, April-May for Chandan Yatra. Note that these periods attract huge crowds.

Weather: October to March offers pleasant weather for temple visits and sightseeing.

Tips for First-Time Visitors

Arrive Early

Arrive at least 30 minutes before the aarti you wish to attend to secure a good viewing position, especially during festivals.

Hire a Guide

Consider hiring an authorized guide to learn about the temple's history, architecture, and rituals. Guides are available at the temple entrance.

Carry Cash

Carry small denominations for offerings, donations, and purchasing prasad. Digital payments may not be accepted inside the temple complex.

Stay Hydrated

Temple visits can be lengthy, especially during festivals. Carry water and stay hydrated, particularly in summer.

Respect Customs

Follow all temple rules and respect the sentiments of fellow devotees. Maintain silence during prayers and rituals.

Plan for Crowds

Expect large crowds, especially on weekends, festivals, and full moon days. Be patient and maintain decorum.

Nearby Attractions

Gundicha Temple

Located 3 km from the main temple, Gundicha Temple is where the deities stay during Rath Yatra. It's known as the "Aunt's Home" of Lord Jagannath.

Mausi Maa Temple

This temple is dedicated to the aunt of Lord Jagannath and is visited during the Rath Yatra return journey.

Narendra Tank

A sacred tank where the Chandan Yatra boat rides take place. It's a peaceful place for meditation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can non-Hindus visit the Jagannath Temple?

A: Non-Hindus are not allowed inside the main temple sanctum but can view the temple from outside and participate in the Rath Yatra festival where they can see the deities.

Q: What is the best time to visit Jagannath Temple?

A: Early morning (5-6 AM) for Mangala Aarti or evening (7-8 PM) for Sandhya Aarti are the best times for a spiritual experience with fewer crowds.

Q: Is there any entry fee for the temple?

A: No, there is no entry fee for the Jagannath Temple. However, donations are accepted.

Q: Can we carry mobile phones inside the temple?

A: No, mobile phones are not allowed inside the temple. Storage facilities are available at the entrance where you can deposit your phone.

Q: How long does a typical temple visit take?

A: A typical visit, including darshan and prasad collection, takes about 1-2 hours. During festivals, it may take longer due to crowds.

Q: Is photography allowed inside the temple?

A: No, photography is strictly prohibited inside the temple complex. You can take photos from outside the temple premises.

Q: What is the Mahaprasad?

A: Mahaprasad is the sacred food offering to Lord Jagannath, distributed to devotees. It's vegetarian, cooked without onion and garlic, and is considered blessed by the deity.

Q: When is the Rath Yatra festival held?

A: Rath Yatra typically occurs in June-July, on the second day of the bright fortnight of Ashadha month. The exact date varies each year according to the Hindu calendar.
